Venus Missions

These once-top-secret missions to Venus have now been declassified, unveiling fascinating insights into Earth's fiery twin planet.

Soviet Venus Missions

  • Venera 1 (1961): The first spacecraft sent to Venus, marking humanity's initial attempt to explore another planet.
  • Venera 7 (1970): The first successful landing on Venus's surface and the first spacecraft to transmit data back from another planet.
  • Venera 9 (1975): Delivered the first-ever images of Venus's surface, revealing its rocky and harsh terrain.
  • Venera 13 (1982): Provided color images and conducted soil analysis, showcasing Venus's extreme conditions.
  • Vega Program (1985): A dual mission that sent probes to study Venus's atmosphere and continued to Halley's Comet.

  • Upcoming Missions to Venus

  • VERITAS (NASA): Planned for launch in the late 2020s, this mission will map Venus's surface with radar to understand its geology and search for evidence of past water.
  • DAVINCI+ (NASA): Aiming to launch in the late 2020s, this mission will analyze Venus's atmosphere to uncover its chemical composition and determine if it had an ocean in the past.
  • EnVision (ESA): Expected in the early 2030s, this European mission will study Venus's surface and subsurface using advanced radar and spectrometry to understand the planet's evolution.
  • Venera-D (Russia): A proposed joint mission between Roscosmos and NASA to study Venus's atmosphere and surface with updated lander technology.
