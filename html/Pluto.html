Pluto:The ostracized One

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the Solar System beyond Neptune filled with icy bodies.Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union because other objects might cross its orbit.

A Brief Description

Pluto has a unique and fascinating appearance shaped by its icy composition, distant location, and atmospheric conditions. Its's surface has mountains, valleys, plains, and craters.
Pluto has five known moons: Charon, Nix, Hydra, Kerberos, and Styx. This moon system might have formed by a collision between Pluto and another similar-sized body early in the history of the solar system.

Size

With a diameter of 2,377 kilometers (1,477 miles),pluto is smaller than Earth's Moon!

Atmosphere-Temperatures

Thin and primarily made of nitrogen, with traces of methane and carbon monoxide, it's atmosphere expands when Pluto is closer to the Sun and freezes when it’s farther away.

Temperature

Pluto becomes much colder during the part of each year when it is traveling far away from the Sun. During this time, the bulk of the planet's atmosphere may freeze and fall as snow to the surface. Temperatures average about -229°C (-380°F).

Exploration History

New Horizons Mission(2006)

Discoveries:

  • Captured stunning high-resolution images of Pluto’s surface, revealing its diverse geology, icy plains, mountains, and atmosphere.
  • Discovered Pluto's five moons in greater detail and provided insights into the composition and dynamics of Pluto's system.
  • Evidence of possible cryovolcanism (ice volcanoes) and a subsurface ocean.

Fun Facts!

  • A day on Pluto (one full rotation) takes about 6.4 Earth days.
  • Pluto has a heart-shaped crater, called Sputnik Planitia!
  • Pluto was named by an 11-year-old girl, Venetia Burney, a schoolgirl from England, which suggested the name "Pluto" after the Roman god of the underworld.
