Welcome to Ceres

• Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter and is classified as a dwarf planet.
• It has a rocky core and an icy mantle and might even have a subsurface ocean of liquid water.
• Ceres was the first asteroid to be discovered, in 1801, by Giuseppe Piazzi.
• The surface features include craters, bright spots made of salts, and evidence of cryovolcanism.
• Ceres has a thin atmosphere that contains water vapor, likely originating from sublimation or cryovolcanic activity.
• It is one of the few places in the solar system that scientists believe could potentially harbor microbial life.

Ceres Information
Diameter 940 km
Mass 9.39 × 10²⁰ kg (0.00015 Earths)
Surface Gravity 0.27 m/s²
Orbital Period 1,680 Earth days
Average Surface Temperature -105°C
Atmosphere Thin, with water vapor
