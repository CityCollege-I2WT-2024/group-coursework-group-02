Mars: The Red Planet


Mars, often called the "Red Planet", is the fourth planet from the Sun and one of Earth's closest neighbors in the solar system.
It is named after the Roman god of war because of its reddish appearance, caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
Mars has captivated humanity for centuries due to its potential for harboring life and its similarities to Earth, such as polar ice caps, seasons, and a day length close to ours.
Mars is much smaller than Earth, with about half the diameter and one-tenth the mass.

Intersting facts


•Mars has dry riverbeds, deltas, and minerals that form in the presence of water, indicating it had liquid water billions of years ago. Frozen water still exists in its polar ice caps and beneath the surface.
•A day on Mars, called a sol, is about 24 hours and 39 minutes, making it the closest to Earth's day length among all the planets.
