Mercury: The "Fastest" Planet

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system, being slightly bigger in size than the Earth's Moon.
It was named after the Roman god Mercury, the fastest god, due to the fact that it orbits the sun with a speed of 48 km/s, completing a full rotation around it in 88 Earth days!
Mercury has the largest core relative to its size of any planet in the solar system. Its iron core makes up about 85% of the planet's radius, which contributes to its high density.

A Brief Description

Mercury's surface resembles that of Earth's Moon, scarred by many impact craters resulting from collisions with meteoroids and comets.Most of Mercury's surface would appear greyish-brown to the human eye.

Size

With a radius of 2,440 kilometers, Mercury is a little more than 1/3 the width of Earth.

Atmosphere-Temperatures

Mercury is one of the few planets that does not have an atmosphere.
It's atmosphere is composed of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium
and it is not strong enough to support weather phenomena because it is simply too thin.

Temperature

The temperatures on Mercury are extreme!

  • During the day, temperatures on the surface can reach 800 degrees Fahrenheit (430 degrees Celsius).
  • Because the planet has no atmosphere to retain that heat, nighttime temperatures on the surface can drop to minus 290 degrees Fahrenheit (minus 180 degrees Celsius).

Exploration History

Mariner 10 (1974-1975)

First Spacecraft to Visit Mercury: Launched by NASA in 1973, Mariner 10 was the first mission to fly by Mercury. Flybys: Mariner 10 conducted three flybys, capturing about 45% of Mercury’s surface.

Discoveries:

  • Mercury has a thin atmosphere (exosphere).
  • Confirmed its large iron core.
  • Mapped craters and other surface features for the first time.

MESSENGER (2004-2015)

Launched in 2004, MESSENGER took over six years to reach Mercury, using flybys of Earth, Venus, and Mercury to adjust its trajectory. Achievements:

  • Mapped 100% of Mercury’s surface in detail.
  • Confirmed the presence of water ice and organic compounds in permanently shadowed craters near Mercury’s poles.
  • Provided evidence that Mercury's magnetic field is generated by its molten outer core.
  • Revealed Mercury's surface is rich in sulfur.
    • End of Mission: MESSENGER’s mission concluded in 2015 when it deliberately crashed into Mercury’s surface after running out of fuel.

Fun Facts!

  • Mercury does not have moons.
  • Despite its extreme heat, Mercury has ice in permanently shadowed craters near its poles, where sunlight never reaches.
  • At certain points on Mercury's surface, the Sun appears to rise, stop, go backward, and then continue rising. This happens due to Mercury's eccentric (oval-shaped) orbit and slow rotation.
