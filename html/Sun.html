Welcome to the Sun

• The Sun is the center of our solar system and is classified as a G-type main-sequence star (G2V).
• It is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%).
• The Sun generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium in its core.
• Its surface temperature is approximately 5,500°C, while the core reaches about 15 million °C.
• Sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections are common phenomena on its surface.
• The Sun's energy drives weather, climate, and supports life on Earth.
